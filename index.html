Recommendations

Family cancels are the dominant leak (~$800,449, 60%+ of the total). Unlike instructor cancels, they can’t be recovered through subs or makeups — only through retention outreach and behavior change. That’s the biggest lever in the plan. Realistic 12-month recovery target: $200–$320K/yr.
1. Family cancels — $800,449
RETENTION · #1 lever · target $150–250K
  • Auto-flag families at 3+ cancels in 30 days OR attendance below 70%. The top-15 list below is the current queue.
  • Frame the conversation as break-vs-churn: offer a structured 4- or 8-week pause with a re-engagement plan instead of letting families silently drift out.
  • Personalized coordinator outreach beats email — especially for high-value families with 20+ cancels YTD.
  • Track: monthly family-cancel count trend + next-class-attendance rate after outreach.
2. Instructor cancels — ~$0 net leak
Supply-side · target $30–60K
  • Smaller bucket than we originally thought — subs + makeups already recover most instructor cancels.
  • Continue sub premium reform + thin-bench recruiting to shave the residual net leak.
  • Finish Scheduling Bots rollout to shrink acceptance latency.
  • Track: emergency answer rate (already in the CX dashboard).
3. Unclassified — $38,032
Data hygiene · target $50–80K after reclassification
  • Standardize override reasons in XP as a small dropdown (Family Absence, Family No-Show, Instructor Out, Game-U Closure, etc.). Free-text is what created the bare-“Absence” misclassification bug that hid family cancels for months.
  • Once tagged, this bucket collapses — most will reclassify to Family.
  • Add a required reason field so this bucket never grows past a small residual.
4. Game-U closures — $93,436
Workflow check · small bucket
  • Mostly holiday closures — families get a reschedule offer, most makeups get taught and billed. Not really a leak in the billing model.
  • Small residual of truly discretionary Game-U cancels; low priority.
Cross-cutting: how these connect to the Break study
The top-15 heaviest cancel families are the pre-break pipeline. Break Study shows ~80% of formal-break students never return — so intercepting families BEFORE the formal break is filed is the highest-leverage move in the whole plan. The retention playbook for bucket #1 is the front-end of Break-study prevention.
Ownership & cadence: Brief refreshes daily. Top-15 family list divided among coordinators for outreach each Monday; results reported at the following huddle. Track cumulative $ recovered against target quarterly.
